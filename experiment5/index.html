Imitate

We chose to imitate a 3-D flower design: . At first, setting up the lighting and the shaders was quite complicated to figure out. The point lighting would at first grey out the flower structure, and in other situations, the flower would be completely dark. After reading through the documentation to better understand the functionality of point light, we realized that we didn’t specify the lighting along the z-axis, which was causing our original bugs. We are excited to experiment with the current output and see how the flower will evolve into something completely different.

Innovate

After implementing the flower, we decided to add in a fun and unique background. We found this example: and wanted to try and add this to our piece. We had some issues combining the background with the flower but we eventually figured it out. Because of the way our sketch is implemented, we had to discard some elements and rethink how we wanted it to look like. Instead of making the background fill up the entire screen with lines, we ended up just having the lines randomly draw onto the screen and rotate around. This way, it didn't mess up the overall feel of the flower and also made the lines look like they were sprouting from the flower.

Reflection

Mansi - I worked on the imitate and integrate portion of the project, trying new techniques and experimenting with the original flower design. I experimented with WEBGL’s offerings to include new shapes and lighting to create more interaction for the user itself. The user is able to zoom in and out of the design, press any key to save an image of the art piece, display more particle emmission by releasing their mouse, and move their mouse around the piece to rotate the art piece. I also experimented with point lighting, trying out different color schemes, angles of light, and volumes of light on a specific region.

Rebecca - I worked mainly on innovate and the reflection portions. I experimented around with combining 2D elements with 3D objects. It was also quite interesting to see what we can do in WEBGL. There were a few issues figuring out how to combine the background nicely with the flower but I eventually figured it out. Originally, we had some other background ideas in mind but they didn’t work well with the WEBGL library. Overall, it was quite interesting experimenting with drawing randomized lines and utilizing random angles through sine/cosine to make the lines rotate around.

We are very happy with the final result and are quite surprised at how our design evolved from a regular flower to one that has sprouting elements coming from the flower. We really enjoyed playing around with 3D objects as well as messing around with the lighting. Changing up the textures and how the overall vibe/look feels was very fun and we feel like we learned a lot from it. We were truly able to make the examples into our own piece. Adding in the background element was also fascinating in that it added a lot to the flower. Originally, the flower felt a bit simplistic so we wanted to add something more to the background. This background eventually turned into something that felt more like a part of the flower than just a background itself. We really loved how the lines looked like they were sprouting from the flower. Even though we ran into some issues of not being able to implement the same look as the example, it actually helped our piece into creating something more different and unique.

One high of the project was that we were able to combine 3D and 2D elements together to create something unique and aesthetically pleasing. We were able to quickly pick up on how 3D objects work and were very happy about how the piece turned out. One low of the project would have to be the issues that popped up when combining elements together. We had a few different backgrounds in mind but because of how we implemented the flower, we couldn’t use those examples. A lot of them did not work well with WEBGL so we had to explore deeper and find something that fits well. Even though we weren’t able to use some backgrounds, it ended up helping us to create something that is truly unique and fits well. From this, we learned that sometimes restrictions/limitations end up helping us create something new and different. It helped guide us into using techniques that we hadn’t considered before which really helped make our piece more successful.
