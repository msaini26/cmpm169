Experiment 4 - Subject/Topic

Imitate

We decided to imitate this example. For this example, we learned how to set up the camera and utilize it as an element of our piece. For the initial setup, the camera settings were relatively straightforward to follow as long as camera permissions were open within the web browser. When the first art mode was created, we displayed the camera video feed changing with the Mandarin characters. In the camera feed image, we put a bright phone screen in a dimly lit room, and the shape of the phone and its excess brightness emittance are shown in the letters themselves. One area we are interested in exploring is the lettering output and what might happen if we change how the art output is created from the video feed itself. In another art mode, the shapes were distored and blue with the phone brightness while the rest of the squares were red.

Integrate

For integration, we wanted to experiment around with overlaying some effects on top of our current example. We found this example of making images captured from a video constantly spin in circles with the mouse as its origin. When trying to implement it, we ran into some issues with detecting which button is pressed as well as adding filters to the video. We decided to make the spinning video appear in the center of the screen when the spacebar is pressed down, instead of following the mouse and showing up on mouse click. After we got that working, we started to play around with some effects.

Innovate

Our intial thought was that showing a plain video of what is being captured is relatively boring. To make it more interesting, we decided to add a filter to the spinning video. We ran into some issues again where the filters were being applied to everything on the canvas, but we eventually figured out how to specify it so that the filter is only being applied to the spinning video.
